My 2025 in clothes

January 9, 2026

Overview:

  • Catalogued every article of clothing in my closet
  • Tracked every outfit I wore throughout 2025 to understand my clothing usage

Below is an infographic detailing what I wore in 2024 versus what was in my closet. View the image in a new tab to see everything in detail.

My goal for 2024 was to wear 80% of my closet, and I hit 69% or 74% (depending on whether you include the clothes I got rid of throughout the year - the 74% figure wasn’t included last year). In 2025 I wore 91%! As I mentioned last year, it’s not realistic for me to wear every single item in a year (I have a lot of formal items, things I bought for Halloween costumes that will get reused at some point, and clothes that I’d wear when doing outdoor work that might not get worn in one single calendar year). So 91% seems pretty good.

I also got rid of 67 things. That’s so big for me! I did acquire a lot too, but actually getting rid of 67 whole clothing items is not something I could have done in 2024.

Beyond the actual numbers, I feel much happier with my closet now. I am still super emotionally attached to everything I own, but I’m getting better at letting go. I still have things that I should get rid of, and I’m working on that slowly.

As an example: I had three Aran sweaters in my closet. Two were very chunky and boxy (not usually a style I gravitate toward) and one was a smidge too small. I literally wore one of them once, and yet I couldn’t get rid of them because they were beautiful, they were given to me as gifts, and I was convinced I would someday make them work. Then on a random day this year, I saw a woman in the street wearing a beautiful Aran sweater that fit her perfectly. She looked so good in it, and it just became immediately clear that I hated how those sweaters look on me, and that they weren’t serving me. As soon as I got home, they went into the to-get-rid-of pile.

Some takeaways:

  • Getting rid of clothes is hard, but keeping clothes I don’t wear is actually harder on me - it makes me feel a bit guilty and anxious.

  • I wore more clothes overall in 2025 than I did in 2026, and I wore more for each season. I got really into layering, so my outfits consisted of more clothes. I also was more social, and so I had more outings where I wanted to wear cute things.

  • My blue M&S shirt was a favorite this year as well as in 2024. You can’t beat a good basic, and this one is such a nice color that I just wear it a lot.

  • I now have 323 items of clothing in my closet. It’s still an insane number, but I haven’t had that few since before I started closet tracking, so I’m really proud of myself. I’ve got a ways to go before that’s a manageble number though.

Final thoughts:

I had a lot of fun tracking my closet throughout the year, and I love making this kind of graphic. It’s very light data analysis, but it’s very personal, and every metric really means something to me.

Data was tracked in Google Sheets.
