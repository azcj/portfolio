My 2024 in clothes

January 1, 2025

Overview:

Below is an infographic detailing what I wore in 2024 versus what was in my closet. View the image in a new tab to see everything in detail.

I started tracking what I wore in 2023, so 2024 was the first full year of data I have. I initially started because I saw someone on YouTube using a specific app, and I thought it looked cool. After a few months, I realized that I wanted access to even more data, so I decided to make my own tracking system in Google Sheets. Every item I have was catalogued, and I logged almost every outfit I wore. I thought I’d wear about 80% of my clothes as it just wasn’t realistic for me to wear every single item in a year (I have a lot of formal items, things I bought for Halloween costumes, and clothes that I’d wear when doing outdoor work that might not get worn in one single calendar year).

There were some surprises:

  • The sheer amount of clothes I own. I mean holy crap - I had 382 items at some point throughout the year (and this isn’t including shoes, workout clothes, or accessories!).

  • What sorts of clothes I actually wear. I don’t wear pants in my life outside of work, but I still didn’t wear all of my skirts or dresses.

  • Generally how few clothes I actually wear. It’s not a lot, and there is room for more purging.

One note on the amount of gifted clothing I get: this is mostly from my mom. When we lived together, going to thrift stores was a hobby we both shared. Now that we live apart, my mom continues to do that and send me whatever she finds that she thinks I’d like. I also get a couple items a year from my partner, but these are always new items that I’ve picked out.

Final thoughts:

  • I own way too much outerwear and could stand to get rid of a lot of it and not miss it.

  • I love second-hand clothes, but my most worn items tend to be new. I think this is because in the past, I haven’t been very picky with thrifted items whereas I am when I buy new. I am working on this mentality, and I am trying to be pickier across the board.

  • I just really like clothes! I have a lot of them, they bring me joy, and it’s so hard for me to get rid of things. One thing that I’ve learned this year is to not purge my closet all at once because that honestly brings me a lot of sadness. Instead, if I decide to wear something and I just don’t love the way it fits on my body that day, I chuck it into a “maybe” pile that then gets reevaluated later so I can donate those things. This way, getting rid of items doesn’t have to feel so sad to me. I am also really sentimental when it comes to clothes. I have t-shirts that I bought when I was a teenager that I just can’t bear to get rid of yet.
